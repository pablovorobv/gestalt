What is the role of the Gestalt therapist?

Responsible for the confidentiality of the information provided by the client. Builds a trusting, secure relationship so that you can open up and immerse yourself in work. Responsible for the organization of the therapy process and for the process itself. If the client does not want to use the acquired knowledge and skills in his life, then the therapist is not responsible for this. Observes the body, posture, gestures, facial expressions, feelings, emotions, the client's words and his own, analyzes this and plans further work. Support and accompaniment of the client on the path of self-exploration. Identifying phenomena and returning them to the client.

Conducting Gestalt Therapy

Gestalt therapy has proven itself well both in personal therapy and in group work. The group has its advantages and limitations. Group work is very different from individual work. It is worth listening carefully to yourself in order to understand which format is closer to you? Most often, work is most effective both in personal therapy and in a group. But not always and not everyone should combine such work. Sometimes the most productive will be only individual work. Advantages of individual work: Working one on one with a Gestalt therapist makes it much easier to open up and work through deep traumas without fear of judgment or rejection. Especially if there is already a trauma of rejection or a breach of trust in the person's personal history; they will work only with your history, with your requests and only at a pace that is convenient for you; work is focused exclusively on you. And if some techniques or techniques did not fit during the work, then the therapist can easily change them and will not use them in the future. In a group format, there is no way to quickly adjust to each participant.
